The Maker

I learned to knit at age six, taught by my father — a prairie homestead child from a family of eight, where knitting was simply something everyone did, brothers and sisters alike. My mother soon took over my education. She had learned as a child in England during World War II, knitting hats for soldiers alongside her schoolmates. She was a beautiful, lightning-fast knitter who never knit a gauge swatch in her life.

Over the years I mastered cables, lacework, colourwork, and countless other techniques — and then discovered felting, quite by accident. That happy accident led me to courses in wet felting, and eventually to my own deep dive into needle felting and fulling, which is technically what I do with all my bags.

Knitting is my sanity, my relaxation, and my creative outlet. I am hopelessly addicted. I have far too many sweaters to ever wear — I live on Vancouver Island, where it rarely gets below zero — but I can't bear to part with a single one. Each one is a labour of love.

Based in Victoria, BC, where I live with my husband and very important dog, Nellie.
